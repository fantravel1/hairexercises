Curly Hair Care Guide

Type 3 hair — defined loops and spirals. Master moisture, gentle detangling, and the techniques that let your curls thrive.

Understanding Curly Hair

Curly hair (Type 3) is defined by springy, well-formed loops and spirals that range from loose ringlets to tight corkscrews. It has a natural bounce and volume that other hair types can't replicate. The curl pattern comes from the shape of the hair follicle — oval or asymmetrical follicles produce curls.

The biggest challenge? Moisture. Because curly hair spirals, the natural oils produced by your scalp can't easily travel down the hair shaft the way they do with straight hair. This means curly hair is naturally prone to dryness, frizz, and breakage. The good news: once you understand how to keep your curls hydrated and handle them gently, Type 3 hair is incredibly rewarding — full of texture, movement, and character.

5-Minute Fast Start Routine

The essential daily routine for curly hair. Follow these steps every wash day to keep your curls defined and hydrated.

1

Co-Wash or Sulfate-Free Shampoo

Cleanse with a co-wash (conditioner-only wash) or a gentle sulfate-free shampoo. Traditional shampoos strip the natural oils that curly hair desperately needs. Focus on the scalp and let the product rinse through the lengths.

2

Generous Conditioner & Finger Detangle

Apply a generous amount of moisturizing conditioner from mid-length to ends. Detangle using only your fingers or a wide-tooth comb, working from the tips upward. Never start from the roots — this causes breakage and disrupts curl pattern.

3

Squish to Condish

With conditioner still in your hair, cup your curls in your palms and squeeze upward toward your scalp. This technique encourages curl formation and helps the conditioner penetrate deep into the hair shaft. You should hear a squelching sound — that's the sign it's working.

4

Microfiber Towel Scrunch

Gently scrunch out excess water with a microfiber towel or a soft cotton t-shirt. Never rub — rubbing disrupts your curl pattern and creates frizz. Scrunching preserves curl definition while removing water.

5

Apply Curl Cream or Leave-In to Wet Hair

While your hair is still soaking wet, apply a curl cream, leave-in conditioner, or both. Wet hair absorbs product much better than damp or dry hair. Use the praying hands method to distribute evenly, then scrunch upward to encourage curls.

Weekly Routine

Beyond your wash-day basics, add these habits to keep curly hair at its healthiest.

★

Deep Conditioning (1x/week)

Apply a deep conditioning mask or treatment once per week. Leave it on for 15-30 minutes under a shower cap for extra penetration. This restores moisture lost throughout the week and keeps curls soft and elastic.

★

Protein Treatment (1x/month)

Curly hair needs a balance of moisture and protein. Once a month, use a protein treatment to strengthen the hair shaft and reduce breakage. Over-doing protein makes hair brittle, so keep it to once monthly.

★

Scalp Massage (3-4x/week)

Spend 4-5 minutes massaging your scalp with your fingertips in gentle circular motions. This boosts blood circulation, supports healthy hair growth, and helps distribute natural oils. Use a lightweight oil like jojoba or argan for added benefit.

Wash Frequency Guide

Curly hair doesn't need frequent washing — over-washing strips the natural oils your curls rely on for moisture and definition.

Recommended: Every 3-5 days

Most men with curly hair do best washing every 3 to 5 days. Between shampoo days, co-wash (rinse with conditioner only) to refresh curls without stripping oils. If your scalp feels itchy between washes, try a lightweight scalp rinse with water and a small amount of conditioner. Training your hair to go longer between washes helps your natural oils build up, leading to healthier, more defined curls over time.

Do's & Don'ts

Do

  • Use leave-in conditioner every single wash day — it's non-negotiable for curly hair
  • Detangle with fingers or a wide-tooth comb only — never a fine-bristle brush
  • Apply styling products to soaking wet hair for maximum absorption and definition
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction, frizz, and breakage overnight
  • Embrace shrinkage — it's a sign of healthy, elastic curls with strong curl pattern

Don't

  • Brush curly hair when dry — this breaks up curl clumps and creates a frizzy mess
  • Use sulfates or harsh shampoos — they strip the natural oils curly hair needs to stay hydrated
  • Touch your curls while they're drying — this disrupts the curl pattern and causes frizz
  • Use terry cloth towels — the rough texture roughs up the cuticle and creates frizz
  • Skip conditioner ever — curly hair without conditioner is a recipe for dryness and tangles

5 Common Mistakes

  1. 1. Brushing curly hair dry. This is the most common mistake. Brushing dry curls separates the curl clumps, destroys definition, and creates a frizzy, puffy mess. Only detangle when hair is wet and saturated with conditioner.
  2. 2. Using the wrong products. Products designed for straight hair — lightweight sprays, volumizing mousses, alcohol-based gels — don't meet the moisture demands of curly hair. Use products specifically formulated for curls with hydrating ingredients.
  3. 3. Touching curls while drying. Every time you touch, scrunch, or fiddle with curls as they dry, you break the gel cast forming around each curl. This leads to undefined, frizzy results. Hands off until fully dry.
  4. 4. Skipping deep conditioning. Curly hair loses moisture faster than any other type. Without weekly deep conditioning, curls become dry, brittle, and lose their definition over time. Make it a non-negotiable weekly habit.
  5. 5. Heat damage from styling tools. Frequent blow drying, flat ironing, or using heat without protectant permanently damages the curl pattern. If you must use heat, always apply a heat protectant and use the lowest effective temperature.

Ingredient Guide for Curly Hair

Know what to look for — and what to avoid — on the label.

Look For

  • Shea butter — deeply moisturizes and helps define curls without weighing them down
  • Coconut oil — penetrates the hair shaft to reduce protein loss and add softness
  • Glycerin — a humectant that draws moisture from the air into your curls
  • Aloe vera — lightweight hydration that soothes the scalp and conditions curls
  • Argan oil — rich in vitamin E and fatty acids, seals in moisture and adds shine

Avoid

  • Sulfates (SLS/SLES) — harsh detergents that strip natural oils and dry out curls
  • Drying alcohols (alcohol denat, isopropyl) — evaporate moisture and leave hair brittle
  • Heavy silicones without clarifying — dimethicone and cyclomethicone build up over time, blocking moisture from entering the hair shaft unless regularly removed with a clarifying wash
