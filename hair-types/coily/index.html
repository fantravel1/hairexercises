Coily Hair Care Guide

Type 4 hair — tight coils, zig-zag patterns, and maximum texture. Learn to lock in moisture, protect your coils, and build a routine that unlocks this hair type's full potential.

Understanding Coily Hair

Coily hair (Type 4) features tight, densely packed coils that range from soft S-shaped coils (4A) to sharp zig-zag patterns (4C). It has the most texture and versatility of any hair type, capable of holding intricate styles and defying gravity in ways no other texture can. Coily hair also has significant shrinkage — it can appear 50-75% shorter than its actual stretched length.

The defining challenge of coily hair is moisture retention. Because the tight coil pattern makes it difficult for natural scalp oils to travel down the hair shaft, coily hair is naturally drier and more fragile than other types. Without consistent moisture and gentle handling, it becomes brittle and prone to breakage. The good news: once you master the moisture-protection cycle, coily hair thrives and rewards you with incredible definition, volume, and style options.

5-Minute Fast Start Routine

The foundation routine for coily hair. On wash days, allow extra time for the deep condition step.

1

Pre-Poo Oil Treatment

Before washing, apply coconut oil or olive oil generously to dry hair. This coats and protects strands from losing moisture during the cleansing process. Leave for at least 10 minutes — or overnight for maximum benefit.

2

Gentle Cleanse

Use a sulfate-free shampoo or co-wash (conditioner-only wash). Focus on the scalp with fingertips — never nails. Let the product rinse through the lengths without scrubbing the fragile coils directly.

3

Deep Condition

Apply a rich, moisture-heavy deep conditioner from root to tip. While the conditioner is in, gently detangle with your fingers — starting from the ends and working upward. Leave for 5-15 minutes.

4

LOC Method (Liquid-Oil-Cream)

On damp hair, layer: Liquid (water or leave-in conditioner), Oil (coconut, castor, or jojoba oil), and Cream (styling cream or shea butter). This layering technique locks moisture into each coil. Some prefer LCO (cream before oil) — experiment to see which works better for your hair.

5

Protective Style & Seal

Style hair in a low-manipulation protective style (twists, braids, or a simple twist-out). At night, seal everything in with a satin bonnet or satin pillowcase to prevent moisture loss and friction damage while you sleep.

Weekly Routine

Add these habits to your weekly cycle to keep coils healthy, hydrated, and strong.

★

Deep Conditioning Treatment (1x/week)

Use a protein-moisture balanced deep conditioner. Apply generously, cover with a plastic cap, and leave for 15-30 minutes. Heat from a warm towel or hooded dryer helps the product penetrate deeper.

★

Scalp Care (2-3x/week)

Massage scalp with a lightweight oil (jojoba or tea tree blend) for 5 minutes using circular motions. This supports blood circulation, helps with dryness, and keeps the scalp environment healthy.

★

Mid-Week Moisture Refresh

Between wash days, spritz hair with a water and leave-in conditioner mix, then seal with a light oil. This prevents the dryness that builds up over several days, especially in low-humidity environments.

★

Protein Treatment (1x/month)

Use a protein treatment or egg-based mask once a month to strengthen coils and reduce breakage. Always follow with a moisturizing conditioner to maintain the protein-moisture balance.

Wash Frequency Guide

Coily hair does not need frequent washing. Over-washing strips the limited natural oils that your coils desperately need. Here's your starting point:

Recommended: Every 5-7 Days (Co-Wash Between)

Most men with coily hair do best with a full shampoo wash every 5-7 days. Between washes, co-wash (conditioner-only wash) to refresh without stripping moisture. If your scalp feels itchy or buildup accumulates faster, a co-wash mid-week is enough — you do not need shampoo every time. On non-wash days, refresh with a water and leave-in spray to reactivate your LOC layers.

Styling Do's & Don'ts

Do

  • Use the LOC or LCO method to layer and lock in moisture
  • Wear protective styles (twists, braids, locs) to reduce manipulation
  • Detangle with fingers only — start from the ends, work upward
  • Deep condition weekly with a moisture-rich treatment
  • Sleep with a satin bonnet or on a satin pillowcase every night
  • Be patient — coily hair rewards consistency over time

Don't

  • Use shampoos with sulfates — they strip essential moisture
  • Comb or brush when dry — this causes breakage and damage
  • Skip moisture — dryness is the biggest enemy of coily hair
  • Use heat frequently — heat damage is often irreversible on coils
  • Over-manipulate — constant styling and restyling causes breakage
  • Sleep on cotton pillowcases — cotton absorbs moisture and creates friction

5 Common Mistakes with Coily Hair

  1. 1. Detangling dry hair. Coily hair should only be detangled when wet and saturated with conditioner. Dry detangling rips through coils, causing breakage and split ends. Use your fingers first, then a wide-tooth comb if needed.
  2. 2. Skipping the LOC method. Water alone evaporates quickly from coily hair. Without the oil and cream layers to seal it in, your hair dries out within hours. The LOC/LCO method is not optional — it's essential.
  3. 3. Washing too often. Coily hair only needs a full shampoo wash once a week at most. Overwashing strips the natural oils that are already scarce due to the tight coil pattern.
  4. 4. Using the wrong products. Products designed for straight or wavy hair are often too lightweight for coily textures. Your hair needs rich creams, butters, and oils — not water-based gels and sprays.
  5. 5. Neglecting scalp health. With less frequent washing, the scalp can accumulate buildup. Regular scalp massages and occasional clarifying treatments keep the scalp environment healthy without over-cleansing the hair.

Ingredient Guide for Coily Hair

Know what to look for — and what to avoid — on the label.

Look For

  • Shea butter — rich moisture that coily hair absorbs and needs
  • Castor oil — seals moisture, supports scalp health, adds shine
  • Coconut oil — penetrates the hair shaft for deep hydration
  • Glycerin — powerful humectant that draws moisture into coils
  • Aloe vera — lightweight hydration and scalp soothing

Avoid / Limit

  • Sulfates (SLS/SLES) — strip the natural oils coily hair desperately needs
  • Mineral oil — coats hair, blocks moisture absorption
  • Drying alcohols (SD alcohol, denatured alcohol) — dehydrate fragile coils
  • Petrolatum — creates a seal that traps dirt and prevents hydration
  • Parabens — potential irritants, especially on sensitive scalps
