Straight Hair

Straight hair lies flat from root to tip with no natural curl pattern, making it one of the most manageable textures but also the most prone to looking oily. The right wash frequency, lightweight styling, and volume-building habits keep straight hair looking clean, fresh, and full of life.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair forms natural S-shaped bends that give it body and movement without the tight curl. It sits at the crossroads of straight and curly, which means it can frizz easily or fall flat depending on your routine. Balancing moisture and hold is the key to bringing out your wave pattern.

Curly Hair

Curly hair grows in defined loops and spirals that are full of personality but demand extra moisture. The natural curl pattern makes it more susceptible to dryness and breakage, which is why gentle detangling, deep conditioning, and protective techniques are non-negotiable for keeping curls healthy and defined.

How to Identify Your Hair Type

Not sure which category fits? Here is a simple breakdown of the four main hair types plus our thinning support track.

🔎 The Strand Test

Wash your hair without any product and let it air dry completely. Do not touch it while it dries. Once fully dry, look at the natural shape of a single strand. This reveals your true hair type without the influence of styling, heat, or product buildup.

⎯

Type 1 — Straight

Your strand lies completely flat with no bends or waves. Hair tends to be shiny because natural oils travel easily from root to tip. Subtypes range from fine and thin (1A) to coarse and thick (1C).

Key focus: Oil control, volume, lightweight care
∿

Type 2 — Wavy

Your strand shows a gentle S-shape or loose bend. Waves may range from barely there (2A) to well-defined with some frizz (2C). This type responds well to scrunching and air drying.

Key focus: Moisture balance, anti-frizz, wave definition
∞

Type 3 — Curly

Your strand wraps around itself in defined loops or spirals. Curls can range from loose ringlets (3A) to tight corkscrews (3C). The curl structure makes this type naturally drier and more fragile.

Key focus: Deep moisture, gentle detangling, curl definition
✿

Type 4 — Coily

Your strand forms tight coils, zig-zag shapes, or dense spirals. This type has the most shrinkage and can appear shorter than it actually is. Ranges from soft coils (4A) to very tight, wiry texture (4C).

Key focus: Moisture retention, protective styling, scalp care
💡

Thinning Support

Thinning is not a type but a condition that can happen to any hair texture. If you notice your part widening, hairline shifting, or increased shedding, our thinning track helps you focus on controllable factors while being honest about what requires medical guidance.

Key focus: Gentle handling, scalp environment, low-risk habits

A note on hair types

Hair typing systems are simplified models. Your hair may not fit perfectly into one category, and that is completely normal. Many people have more than one texture across different areas of their head. Use these guides as starting points, not rigid labels. Adjust your routine based on how your hair actually responds.
