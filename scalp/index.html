Oily Scalp

An oily scalp produces excess sebum, leaving hair feeling greasy and flat within a day of washing. This is one of the most common scalp concerns, especially for men with straight or fine hair. The right wash frequency, gentle cleansing, and lifestyle adjustments can help restore balance without stripping your scalp dry.

2-3 day wash cycle
Sulfate-free cleansing
Weekly clarify

Dry Scalp

A dry scalp feels tight, itchy, and may produce small white flakes. Unlike dandruff (which is caused by a fungal overgrowth), dry scalp results from insufficient moisture. Cold weather, overwashing, harsh products, and dehydration are common culprits. Restoring moisture through gentle care and hydration can bring significant relief.

Less frequent washing
Moisturizing shampoo
Scalp oil treatments

Sensitive Scalp

A sensitive scalp reacts easily to products, temperature changes, and environmental factors. Symptoms include redness, stinging, burning, or itching. Fragrance-free, hypoallergenic products and gentle handling are essential for keeping a sensitive scalp calm and comfortable.

Fragrance-free products
Patch test always
Lukewarm water

Dandruff & Flaking

Visible white or yellowish flakes on the scalp that can range from mild to severe. Dandruff can result from Malassezia overgrowth, dry scalp, or other conditions. Anti-dandruff treatments, gentle cleansing, and scalp health optimization can effectively manage flaking.

Anti-dandruff shampoo
2-3x weekly use
Gentle cleansing

Scalp Itch

Persistent itching creates the urge to scratch, which can damage the scalp further. The itch-scratch cycle perpetuates irritation. Breaking this cycle through trigger identification, cool compresses, and gentle care brings relief.

Cool compresses for relief
Tap gently instead of scratch
Identify triggers

Scalp Inflammation

Redness, swelling, and sometimes pain or tenderness indicate scalp inflammation. This occurs when the immune system responds to a trigger: product irritation, infection, or underlying conditions. Anti-inflammatory care and trigger elimination support healing.

Anti-inflammatory approach
Cool compresses daily
Fragrance-free products
Important Guidance

When to See a Professional

Most scalp concerns can be managed with safe hygiene habits. However, some symptoms warrant professional attention.

See a dermatologist or healthcare professional if you experience:

  • Persistent itching, flaking, or redness that does not improve after 2-4 weeks of gentle care
  • Scalp pain, burning, or tenderness that occurs without an obvious cause
  • Sudden or patchy hair loss, or noticeable thinning in specific areas
  • Sores, scabs, or crusty patches on the scalp that do not heal
  • Excessive hair shedding (more than 100 hairs per day on a consistent basis)
  • Any scalp condition that causes significant discomfort or affects your quality of life
